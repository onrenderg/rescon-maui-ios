Development

Buildings should be sufficiently away from steep slopes.
On the valley side, the clearance should be such that the base of the foundation rests on firm soil or rock, and not on filled-up ground.
Foundations should rest only on firm soil and not on filled-up soil.
A minimum clearance of 1.5 m between the toe of the wall and the building wall should be provided.

Buildings should not be constructed too close to other buildings, as there might be a possibility of hammering each other or collapsing during an earthquake.
The distance between any two buildings should be about 1.25m/storey (NBC Norms).
