Masonry in Superstructure

The thickness of the walls depends upon the design...

1. At the site, store bricks in stacks. On the day before using them, spray water to drench the stack.
2. Spray water again before using the bricks. Dry bricks result in low-quality masonry.
3. The bricks are generally laid in English Bond. Wall thickness is equal to one length of the brick.

Step 3: Masonry in Superstructure

● We will make 9” thick masonry walls with 1:6 cement-sand mortar over the RCC Plinth Band.
