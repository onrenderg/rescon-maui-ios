Common Practices for Construction (Initial Phase)

● Draw a 4-meter line from point A to B where a 90-degree angle is required. Fix pegs at points A and B.
● The first person holds the initial end of the measuring tape at peg A at zero and at 12 meters.
● The other person holds the tape at the 4-meter mark and places a peg at point B.
● A third person holds the tape at the 9-meter mark, identifying point C, and then places a peg at C.
● Extend line AC to the required length.
● Clean the construction site and ensure it is flat.
● Place concrete block markers at all corners, about 1.5 meters away from the outer walls of the proposed building.
● Ensure blocks are of equal height to keep the thread off the ground.
● Mark the center line of the outer wall with a cotton thread and tape. Stretch the thread firmly so it does not sag.
● At the building corners, divide the line of cotton thread at right angles using the 3:4:5 method to obtain 90 degrees.

Water Level for Site Leveling

● Use a 10-meter water tube filled with water.
● Hold both ends vertically to prevent water from draining, tapping the tube to remove any trapped air bubbles.
● Ensure that the water level is the same in both ends when held together.
● Identify the reference point A, the level to be transferred to another point B.
● Have an assistant hold the tube at reference level A.
● Place the other end of the tube near point B, adjusting it until the water level matches at A, then mark this level at B.
● Now points A and B are at the same level.
