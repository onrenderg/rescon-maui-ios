Form and Orientation

While planning the layout for your house, there are a few guidelines that should be kept in mind:

Simple shapes perform the best during earthquakes. Make sure that your building has as simple a form as possible. The building shape should be regular. If unavoidable, give separation gaps to form regular shapes.

It is preferable to orient a building in a north-south orientation. The longer side should be on the North-South axis.

• A building should not be too long compared to its width. (The ratio of length to width should not be more than 2:1 or according to local area norms).
• It is better to construct a large building in smaller sections by providing gaps between different sections.
• A symmetrically designed building, both horizontally and vertically, performs better during an earthquake.
