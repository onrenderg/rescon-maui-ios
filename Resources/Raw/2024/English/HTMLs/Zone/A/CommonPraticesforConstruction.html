Common Practices for Construction (Initial Phase)

● Draw a 4 m line from A to B where a 90-degree angle is required. Fix pegs at points A and B.
● The first person holds the initial end of the measuring tape at peg A (zero mark) and also at the 12 m mark.
● The second person holds the tape at the 4 m mark and fixes a peg at B, marking it at 4 m.
● A third person holds the tape at the 9 m mark and places a peg at point C.
● Extend line AC to the required length as needed.
● Clear the construction site and ensure that it is flat.
● Place concrete block markers at all corners, approximately 1.5 m away from the outer walls of the proposed building.
● Ensure blocks are of equal height to keep the thread off the ground.
● Mark the center line of the outer wall with cotton thread and tape. Stretch the thread firmly to prevent sagging.
● At the building corners, align the thread at right angles using the 3:4:5 method to achieve a 90-degree angle.

Water Level for Site Leveling

● Use a water tube approximately 10 m in length, filling it with water.
● Hold both ends vertically to prevent water from draining. Tap the tube to eliminate any trapped air bubbles.
● Check that the water level in both ends of the tube is equal.
● Identify the reference point A, which is the level you wish to transfer to point B.
● Have an assistant stand at reference level A, marking it.
● Move the other end of the tube to point B, adjusting it up and down until the water level matches A's mark.
● Once level, mark point B. Now points A and B are at the same level.
