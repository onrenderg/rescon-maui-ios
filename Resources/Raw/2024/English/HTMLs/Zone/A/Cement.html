Cement

1. Check the manufacturer's name and trademark for accuracy to avoid counterfeit products.
2. Look for the ISI mark in black, ensuring the cement meets standards.
3. Confirm the grade and type, like OPC 43, clearly labeled on the bag.
4. Verify the net weight as stated.
5. Choose a reputed brand and review provided technical details.
6. Check the Maximum Retail Price (MRP) to prevent overcharging.
7. Look for the "Use No Hooks" symbol to avoid damaging the bag.
8. Ensure freshness by checking the batch/control unit number; ideally, it should be less than 3 months old.
9. The manufacturer's address should be listed for inquiries.
10. If performance improvers are added, they should be listed on the bag.
11. Look for the relevant IS code for the cement type (e.g., IS 269 for OPC grades, IS 1489 for PPC).
